Dear life,

Oh what a life. What are you? if not а beast in a costume, or a wedding gown. with your hair dyed all red and bright, oh is that my blood? on your hands, your teeth, what an affectionate kiss. On your hands, stains of a pink lemonade that you handed me in a fancy glass. I thought life was supposed
to give lemons. What a strange life you are. Well i took a sip. Just a sip. a drop down my throat that rendered me high. Ever the highest. I walked through the streets swaying as the wind swayed along. I danced with the whirls. They played with my hair, they tickled my curls. What a delicate touch. silly silly whirls, they thought they were only being playful. Now my hair is a mess. the whirls pushed harder and I tripped, the glass from my hands slipped. now my dress is a mess. I cried. Who am I to impress?. You gave me a big red ribbon. You said I'm just a girl. Too small to be noticed anyway. You said you're not the villain. But i knew, the little girl knew she was the prey. I noticed,yet i kept my heart open. I thought i was the one,the chosen. But here i am next to the glass, just as broken. My red ribbon stolen. Yeah you took it and ran away. Left me stranged, astray. I cried and i cried. Getting up i tried. The young girl, the once innocent, the starry-eyed. Now wailing,frailing, she's burnt no she's fried. Oh i cried and i cried. For your aide. But mere poison! Was your pink lemonade. on me, you preyed. You preyed and you prayed and you prayed.
